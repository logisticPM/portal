# Client Check-in Questionnaire — Indigenomics

**Purpose:** unblock scope. These questions turn the team's ambiguity into client decisions, and let their answers break the tie between candidate directions. Send before, or use live in the check-in. (Mirrors the six asks from Deck 4, slide 19, sharpened to this sprint's decision.)

## A. Positioning & direction
1. Does a **public-facing companion to Indigenomics AI** (complement, not duplicate) match how you envision the platform's growth?
2. We're weighing two concrete first products: **(a) a Reconciliation Hub** — a citation-first, public education tool over open data (the Indigenous economy, procurement, the legal/policy record, real case studies); and **(b)** extending our **consent / data-sovereignty tooling**. Which is more useful to you in the next 6–12 months — and why?
3. Of the deck's directions (simulation, conversational entry, historical layer, reference layer), which one, if we could only ship one well by August, would you most want to see?

## B. Data & sovereignty
4. For the simulation specifically: can you supply or point us to **modellable data for food systems and forest-carbon**? (Housing and energy already have strong open Vancouver data; if these two are thin, we'd focus there.)
5. What **sovereignty, data-residency, and IP constraints** must we design within from day one (OCAP®, hosting, where data may live)?
6. Are there sources or case studies you'd want **included or avoided** in a citation-first knowledge base?

## C. People & cultural authorship
7. Can you **endorse and connect us to advisors** — at minimum one cultural and one economic — and what's the right review cadence with them?
8. Which Nations should we engage first, and through what protocol, for any Nation-specific content?

## D. Scope, timeline & alignment
9. How can the capstone concretely serve the **2026 Indian Act Economics Campaign** and the Vancouver event (timing, audience, format)?
10. Could we explore being the **public face of the TELUS responsible-AI partnership** / hosting on the Sovereign AI Factory — or is that premature?
11. After the capstone: who **owns and hosts** the artifact, and who is our standing **point of contact**?
